Silence, Shame, and Stigma

A Cross-Cultural Look at Mental Health

Exploring the complex intersection of culture, tradition, and mental health through the lens of South Asian and Western perspectives.

About the Topic

Cultural Context

Mental health perception varies dramatically across cultures, with each society developing unique frameworks for understanding psychological well-being. This research examines the profound differences between South Asian and Western approaches to mental health.

Key Concepts

  • Izzat (Honor): The concept of family and personal honor in South Asian cultures
  • Family Duty: Collective responsibility and individual sacrifice
  • Stigma: Social shame and discrimination surrounding mental illness
  • Cultural Identity: How cultural background shapes mental health narratives

Inquiry Question

"How do cultural concepts of honor, family duty, and social stigma influence the perception, treatment, and lived experience of mental health issues in South Asian communities compared to Western societies?"

Sub-Questions for Exploration

  • What role does family honor (izzat) play in mental health disclosure?
  • How do collectivist vs. individualist values affect treatment seeking?
  • What are the generational differences in mental health attitudes?
  • How do religious and spiritual beliefs influence mental health perspectives?
